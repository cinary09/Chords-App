Chord basics

What is a chord?

A chord is multiple notes played together. On guitar, chord diagrams show exactly where your fingers go and which strings should ring.

How chord diagrams work

The six vertical lines are guitar strings. The left line is the low E string, and the right line is the high E string. The horizontal lines are frets.

A black dot shows a fretted note. The number inside the dot tells you which finger to use: 1 index, 2 middle, 3 ring, and 4 pinky.

An O above a string means play it open. An X means mute or skip that string. These small markings are just as important as the finger dots.

Practice checklist

  • Place fingertips close to the frets, not directly on top of them.
  • Keep fingers curved so nearby strings can ring.
  • Pick each string before strumming the whole chord.
  • Practice two-chord changes before full songs.
